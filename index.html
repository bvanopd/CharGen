          Traditions

            Note: A deft character may choose a single tradition focus at character creation. Doing so causes them to lose the Expertise ability.

            Note: A martial character may choose a single tradition focus at character creation. Doing so causes them to lose the Killing Blow and Battlemaster abilities.

            Note: An Arcane character must choose 2 tradition foci at character creation.They can take one tradition to level 2, or choose two traditions at level 1.

            Note: A Devout character must choose 2 tradition foci at character creation.They can take one tradition to level 2, or choose two traditions at level 1.

              Further limitations for traditions are:
            • High Mage cannot be combined with any Devout traditions.
            • Mageslayer can only be combined with Devout traditions, Duelist, and Beastmaster.

            Focus List

            Martial
            Armored Magic
              Rank 1
            • You can cast spells or channel miracles while wearing armor that has an Encumbrance value of no more than two.
            • You can use a shield, provided your other hand is empty for gesturing.
              Rank 2
            • You can cast spells or channel miracles wearing armor of any encumbrance.
            • You can cast spells while your hands are full, though not while bound.
            Armsmaster
              Rank 1
            • Gain melee as a bonus skill.
            • You can ready a Stowed melee or thrown weapon as an Instant action.
            • Add your Melee skill to melee or thrown weapons' damage and shock damage, assuming it has any to begin with.
              Rank 2
            • Add your Melee skill to the armor penetration of your melee attacks. If another ability grants armor penetration, use the highest value among them.
            • Gain a +1 bonus to Attack with all thrown and melee weapons.
            Assassin
              Rank 1
            • Gain Sneak as a bonus skill.
            • You can conceal an object no larger than a knife from anything less than a strip search. You can draw or produce this object as a Free action, and your point-blank thrown or melee attacks made during a surprise round with it cannot miss the target.
              Rank 2
            • You can take a Move action on the same round you make an Execution attack. You may split this movement around the attack. This movement happens too quickly to alert a victim or to be hindered by bodyguards.
            Close Combatant
              Rank 1
            • Gain any combat skill as a bonus skill.
            • You can use thrown weapons in melee without suffering penalties from melee attackers.
            • You ignore Shock damage from melee assailants, but invoking this benefit disrupts any spellcasting this round.
              Rank 2
            • Add half of your level (rounded up) to the armor penetration of your melee attacks. If another ability grants armor penetration, use the highest value among them.
            • The Fighting Withdrawal combat action is a Free action for you and can be performed freely.
            Die Hard
              Rank 1
            • Gain 2 additional maximum hit points per level.
            • When rolling for Death and Dismemberment, increase your CON modifier by +2, up to a maximum of 4.
              Rank 2
            • The first time each day that you are reduced to zero hit points by an injury, you instead survive with one hit point. This can't save you from large-scale, instantly-lethal trauma.
            Deadeye
              Rank 1
            • Gain Ranged as a bonus skill.
            • You can ready a Stowed ranged weapon as an Instant action.
            • You may use a two-handed ranged weapon even when an enemy is within melee range, at a -4 penalty.
            • Add your Ranged skill to damage with ranged weapons.
              Rank 2
            • You can reload slow-loading weapons as an Instant action, provided they don't take more than a round to reload.
            • You can use ranged weapons of any size in melee without penalty.
            • Once per scene, as a Free Action when shooting at a non-creature target, you automatically hit unless the shot is physically impossible.
            Impervious Defense
              Rank 1
            • You have an innate Armor of 13 plus half your character level, rounded up.
              Rank 2
            • Once per day, as an Instant action, you can shrug off any single weapon attack or physical trauma inflicted by a foe. Environmental damage, falling damage, or other harm that couldn't be forfended by strong armor cannot be resisted this way.
            Shocking Assault
              Rank 1
            • Gain Unarmed or Melee as a bonus Skill.
            • Add half of your level (rounded up) to the armor penetration of your melee attacks. If another ability grants armor penetration, use the highest value among them.
              Rank 2
            • Gain a +2 bonus to the Shock damage rating of all the melee weapons and unarmed attacks that do Shock.
            Sniper's Eye
              Rank 1
            • Gain Ranged as a bonus skill.
            • When making a skill check for an Execution Attack with a ranged weapon or target shooting, roll 3d6 and drop the lowest die.
              Rank 2
            • You don't miss ranged Execution Attacks. A target hit by one takes a -4 penalty on the Physical saving throw to avoid immediate mortal injury. Even if the save is successful, the target takes double the normal damage inflicted by the attack.
            Unarmed Combatant
              Rank 1
            • Gain Unarmed as a bonus skill.
            • Your unarmed attacks become more dangerous as your Unarmed skill increases. At level-0, they do 1d6 damage. At level-1, they do 1d8 damage. At level-2 they do 1d10, level-3 does 1d12, and level-4 does 1d12+1.
            • At Unarmed-1 or better, they have the Shock quality, with damage equal to your Unarmed skill and an armor penetration of 4.
              Rank 2
            • Even on a miss with an Unarmed attack, you do an unmodified 1d6 damage, plus any Shock that the blow might inflict on the target.
            Valiant Defender
              Rank 1
            • Gain Melee or Unarmed as a bonus skill.
            • Gain a +2 on all skill checks for the Screen Ally combat action.
            • You can screen against one more attacker per round than your skill would normally allow.
            • Once per round, you can Screen Ally against even intangible spells or magical attacks or bodily shield them from an area-effect explosion or magic. Such attempts require the usual successful opposing skill check, with the assailant using their Magic skill.
              Rank 2
            • The first Screen Ally skill check you make in a round is always successful.
            • Gain +2 Defense while screening someone.
            • You can screen against foes as large as ogres or oxen.
            Whirlwind Assault
              Rank 1
            • Gain Melee as a bonus skill.
            • Once per scene, as a Free action, apply your Shock damage to all foes within melee range, assuming they're susceptible to your Shock.
              Rank 2
            • The first time you kill someone in a round with a normal attack, either with its rolled damage on a hit or with the Shock damage it inflicts, instantly gain a second attack on any target within range using any Ready weapon you have.
            Deft
            Alert
              Rank 1
            • Gain Notice as a bonus skill
            • You cannot be surprised or targeted by execution attacks.
            • Roll initiative with advantage.
              Rank 2
            • You always win initiative unless someone else involved is also this alert.
            Authority
              Rank 1
            • Gain Lead as a bonus skill.
            • Once per day, you can make a request from an NPC who is not openly hostile, rolling a Cha/Lead skill check at a difficulty of the NPC's Morale score. If you succeed, they will comply with the request, provided it is not significantly harmful or uncharactaristic.
              Rank 2
            • Any NPC being directly led by you gains a Moral and Attack bonus equal to your Lead skill, and a +1 on all skill checks. Your followers and henchmen will not act against your interests unless under extreme pressure.
            Connected
              Rank 1
            • Gain Connect as a bonus skill.
            • If you've spent at least a week in a not-entirely-hostile location, you'll have built a web of contacts willing to do favors for you that are no more than mildly illegal. You can call on one favor per game day and the GM decides how far they'll go for you.
              Rank 2
            • Once per game session, if it's not entirely implausible, you meet someone you know who is willing to do modest favors for you. You can decide when and where you want to meet this person, but the GM decides who they are and what they can do for you.
            Cultured
              Rank 1
            • Gain Connect as a bonus skill.
            • You can fluently speak all the common languages of your native region and can convey at least basic information in the uncommon ones.
            • You can learn a new language with only a week's practice with a native speaker.
            • Once per game day, your polished ways automatically gain a minor favor from an NPC that would not put them to significant expense or risk, assuming the NPC isn't hostile to you.
              Rank 2
            • Once per game session, reroll a failed social skill check.
            Dealmaker
              Rank 1
            • Gain Trade as a bonus skill.
            • With a half hour of effort you can find a buyer or seller for any good or service that can be traded in the community, legal or otherwise.
              Rank 2
            • Once per game session, target a sentient who is not just then trying to kill you or your allies and make a request of it that it can comprehend. If it's at all possible for it to make such terms, it will do so for a price or favor it thinks you can grant, though the price for significant favors might be dear.
            Diplomatic Grace
              Rank 1
            • Gain Convince as a bonus skill.
            • You speak all the languages common to your region of the world and can learn new ones to a workable level in a week, becoming fluent in a month.
            • Reroll 1s on any skill check related to negotiation or diplomacy.
              Rank 2
            • Once per day, silenty consecrate a bargain; the target must make a Mental save to break the deal unless their life or something they love as much is imperiled by it. Most NPC's won't even try to break it. The deal must be fore something specific and time-limited, and not an open-ended bargain.
            Gifted Surgeon
              Rank 1
            • Gain Heal as a bonus skill.
            • You may attempt to stabilize one Mortally Wounded adjacent person per round as a Move Action.
            • When rolling Heal skill checks, roll 3d6 and drop the lowest die.
            • You heal twice as many hit points as usual when apply first aid.
              Rank 2
            • Your curative efforts count as magical healing. You can heal 1d6+Heal Skill in damage to an adjacent Mortally Wounded ally as a Main Action, potentially reviving them. Each such application of helaing adds 1 System Strain to the target.
            Henchkeeper
              Rank 1
            • Gain Lead as a bonus skill.
            • You can acquire henchmen within 24 hours of arriving in a community, assuming anyone is suitable hench material. These henchmen will not fight except to save their own lives, but will escort you on adventures and risk great danger to help you. Most henchmen from a civilized society will be treated as Peaceful Humans from the bestiary. You can have one henchmen at a time for every three character levels you have, rounded up. You can release henchmen with no hard feelings at any plausible time and pick them back up later should you be without a current henchmen.
              Rank 2
            • Your henchmen are remarkably loyal and determind, and will fight for you against anything but clearly overwhelming odds. They're treated as Veteran Soldier from the bestiary. You can make faithful henchmen out of skilled and highly-capable NPC's but this requires that you actually have done them some favor or help that would reasonably earn such fierce loyalty.
            Imposter
              Rank 1
            • Gain Perform or Sneak as a bonus skill.
            • Once per scene, reroll any failed skill check or saving throw related to maintaining an imposture or disguise.
            • Create one false identity of no great social importance; you can flawlessly pretend to be that person, such that only exteme persuasive proof can connnect you with it. You can acquire.
            • You heal twice as many hit points as usual when apply first aid.
              Rank 2
            • Your curative efforts count as magical healing. You can heal 1d6+Heal Skill in damage to an adjacent Mortally Wounded ally as a Main Action, potentially reviving them. Each such application of helaing adds 1 System Strain to the target.
            Poisoner
              Rank 1
            • Gain Heal as a bonus skill.
            • Your toxins inflict 2d6 damage plus your level on a hit or Shock, with a Physical save for half. Your incapacitating or hallucinogenic toxins do the same, but those reduced to zero hit points are simply incapacitated for an hour.
              Rank 2
            • You are immune to poison and can applly a universal antidote to any poisoned ally as a Main Action.
            • Any attempt to detect or save against your poisons takes a penalty equal to your Heal skill.
            • Your ingested poisons count as an Execution Attack against unsuspecting targets with Heal used for the Physical saving throw penalty and 1d6 damage per level done on a success. Such poisons can be non-lethal at your discretion.
            Polymath
              Rank 1
            • Gain any one bonus skill.
            • You treat all non-combat skills as if they were at least level-0 for purposes of skill checks, even if you lack them entirely.
              Rank 2
            • You treat all non-combat skills as if they were at least level-1 for purposes of skill checks.
            Rider
              Rank 1
            • Gain Ride as a bonus skill.
            • Your steeds all count as Morale 12 in battle, use your Defense if it's higher than theirs, and have 50% more Hex Points of overland movement.
            • You can intuitively communicate with riding beasts, gaining as much information from it as its intellect can convey.
              Rank 2
            • Once per scene, negate a successful attack against your steed as an Instant Action.
            • Once per scene, re-roll any failed Ride skill check.
            • You can telepathically send and receive simple warnings, thoughts, and commands to and from your steed so long as it's within two hundred feet. You can so bond with one steed at a time, taking an hour to do so.
            Specialist
              Rank 1
            • Gain any one skill as a bonus skill.
            • For your chosen skill, roll 3d6 and drop the lowest die for all skill checks.
            • You may choose this Focus more than once to improve different skills.
              Rank 2
            • Roll 4d6 and drop the two lowest dice for all Skill checks with your chosen skill.
            Trapmaster
              Rank 1
            • Gain Notice as a bonus skill.
            • Once per scene, reroll any failed saving throw or skill check related to traps or snares.
            • Given five minutes of work you can trap a portal, container, passageway, or other relatively narrow space with foot snares, caltrops, toxic needles, or other hazards. Non-lethal traps cause the first victim to trigger it to lose a round of actions while dangerous ones inflict 1d6 damage plus twice the character's level, with an appropriate saving throw for half. Only one such improvised trap can be maintained at a time. More fearsome traps may be laid with congenial circumstances and the GM's permission.
              Rank 2
            • You know secrets for unraveling even magical traps or arcane hazards that would normally require a wizard to dispel them. Once per scene, your efforts count as an Dispel Magic spell against the trap or hazard, cast as if a Mage of twice your level, with any relevant skill check being Int/Notice or Dex/Notice. This ability can be used against any stationary magical effect that's susceptible to being dispelled by Dispel Magic
            Well Met
              Rank 1
            • Reaction rolls made by those the party meets are given a +1 bonus so long as you are present, whether or not you do the talking. Even hostile encountered beings will usually give the party a round to parley before attacking unless they're in ambush or have a clear reason for immediate violence.
              Rank 2
            • Once per game session, when a reaction roll is made, cause the subject to be as friendly and helpful to you and your party as it's plausibly possible for them to be. It's up to the GM to decide why the creature becomes so; it might be mistaken about your nature, or find you hilarious, or perhaps want a favor from you and your allies.
            General
            Developed Attribute
              Rank 1
            • Choose an attribute; its modifier is increased by +1, up to a maximum of +3. The score does not increase.
            • You may choose this Focus more than once to improve different attributes.
            Nullifier
              Rank 1
            • You and all allies within twenty feet gain a +2 bonus to all saving throws against magical effects.
            • As a Free Action, you can feel the presence or use of magic within twenty feet of you, though you can't discern detail about it or the specific source.
            • The first failed saving throw against a magical effect you suffer in a day is turned into a success.
              Rank 2
            • Once per day as an Instant Action, you are simply not affected by an unwanted magical effect or supernatural monstrous ability, even if it wouldn't normally allow a saving throw. Immunity to a persistent effect lasts for the rest of the scene.
            Spirit Familiar
              Rank 1
            • Choose a form for your familiar no smaller than a cat nor larger than a human. It has the traits and abilities of an entity created by Find Familiar but may be summoned or dismissed as a Main Action, appearing within melee range of its owner. It cannot carry objects with it during its vanishment aside from the clothing natural to its shape. It has no need for food, water, or sleep. If killed, it vanishes and cannot be re-summoned for 24 hours. Once per day, it can refresh one point of Committed Effort for you.
              Rank 2
            • Pick two benefits from the list below for your familiar. This level may be taken more than once, adding two additional options each time.
            • It has hit points equal to three times your level
            • It gains the ability to attack with a hit bonus equal to half your level, rounded up, doing 1d8 damage on a hit with no Shock
            • It gains a +1 skill check bonus and can apply it to a range of situations equivalent to one normal human background
            • It gains another shape of your choice which it can adopt or discard as a Free action
            • It can hover or fly at its usual movement rate
            • It can communicate freely with others in any language you know
